Biography





John holds a Bachelor of Arts in Economics and Minor in Legal Studies from the University of California Santa Cruz. He was awarded a Juris Doctorat from Southwestern Law School and, while there, he earned Dean's List Honors and served as an Associate Editor of the Southwestern Law Review.


John's legal career began by working for the federal and state courts. In law school, he served as judicial extern to Arthur Alarcon at the Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals and he also externed for Theodore Albert at the United States Bankruptcy Court, Central District of California. Following law school, John earned the prestigious honor of clerking for then-Chief Justice Mark Gibbons at the Nevada Supreme Court. In this capacity, John drafted opinions for all seven members of the Court.


The lessons and insights that John learned from working for the judiciary have proven to be invaluable. After he left the Courts, John joined large firms in San Francisco and Las Vegas and represented multinational insurers and corporations. With the aim of rebalancing his practice, John has taken his corporate training and now uses it to represent individual clients in California and Nevada. His practice focuses on criminal defense and personal injury. From time to time, John likes to tackle other legal issues as well, especially in the areas of insurance disputes, contract disputes, and probate matters. He regularly appears before state, federal, and tribal courts, and his practice includes trials and appeals.
